I've inserted some clips below to allow you to see what hockey being played looks like

  • This video shows a penalty corner. This is awarded to a team when the defender fouls a player on the opposing team within the shooting circle around the goal. It can also be awarded if the defender intentionally hits the ball over the end line.
  • The penalty corner is started when the attacker places the ball on the back line. The penalty corner player must stand at least one foot outside the back line and can either push or knock the ball into play. The ball cannot be lifted or lofted on purpose.
  • As we can see in the video 4 defenders are place in the goal with the goalie. All of their feet and sticks must be behind the goalies line. Each defender wears a face-mask for protection during the penalty corner. The rest of defenders must be behind the 25 yard line. All the attackers must be standing on the arc of the shooting circle and not allowed have their feet or sticks within the circle. Once the ball has been hit or the play has begun and all other players are allowed move from either the inside of the goal or the halfway line.
  • A shot into the goal cannot be taken until the ball has left the shooting circle. Usually the player on the end line pushes the ball towards the attacker at the mt arc of the circle. As soon as a defender sees the ball move they usually run toward the main attacker who the ball is being pushed to to try defend the goal. The attacker will stop the ball outside the circle to allow the next attacker to strike into the goal. The ball must've gone outside the circle before that attacker could strike.
