Rules of hockey

  • Only 10 field players plus a goalie play at one time
  • Hockey players can only hit the ball with the flat side of their stick.
  • Substitutions –a new player step onto the field when the old player has exited the field at the 50
  • Hockey players are not allowed to use their feet, or any other parts of the body to hit or control the ball
  • You must be wearing the appropriate equipment – shin guards, mouth guards, etc
  • A field goal, a penalty corner, or a penalty stroke are the only ways to score a goal. A field goal is a goal scored in open play from inside the'striking circle,' directly in front of the opponent's goal. It does not count as a goal if the hockey ball is hit from outside the circle and goes into the goal.

Fouls of hockey

  • Undercutting : This penalty is given to a player who elevates the ball dangerously.
  • Advancing : This is given to a player who uses any part of their body to shove, push, or advance the ball in any way..
  • Obstruction : This is given to a hockey player who blocks an opponent from reaching the ball with their body or stick.
  • Sticks : This is given to a player that lifts his or her stick dangerously close to another player.
  • Backstick : This is given to a player that strikes the ball with his or her hockey stick's rounded back.
  • Interference : Interference from a hockey stick. This is given to a player who purposefully or unintentionally hits an opponent's stick with their stick

Umpires

During a match there is an umpire on the field. There are three cards that can be given out if a player breaks the rules : green, yellow or red

  • GREEN: an official warning to players who've broken the rules with minor offenses
  • YELLOW: used to remove a player from the game for a minimum of five minutes
  • RED: prevents a player from playing for the remainder of the match
